Understanding Web Accessibility

What is Web Accessibility?

Web accessibility ensures that websites, tools, and technologies are designed and developed so that people with disabilities can use them. This includes individuals with visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, and neurological disabilities.

The Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI), part of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), develops guidelines and resources to make the web more accessible. Their Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are widely adopted standards for accessibility.

Why It Matters

Accessibility benefits everyone, not just those with disabilities. It improves user experience, ensures legal compliance, and expands your audience. For example, captions for videos help not only deaf users but also those in noisy environments.
